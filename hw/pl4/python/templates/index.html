About Me!



I'm a junior at CSU Sacramento who transferred from community college this fall. I love all things tech, finance, cars, photography and sports. I started up a Data Science club here at Sac State to connect with like minded students and work on some cool projects together.

I'm currently working as an intern at Velocity Venture Capital, a VC here in Sacramento. Our goal is to grow the tech scene within the region and invest in exciting companies that solve a series of global challenges.

This summer, I'll be working as a software engineering intern for the Zillow Group in San Francisco, on the Agent Platform team at Trulia.

I also helped co-found AirQuail on changing the way we interact with air quality. We were able to raise $100k in seed funding but didn't move forward with the investment, nevertheless, check us out!

Previously I interned at Lumo BodyTech a wearable startup in Palo Alto, where I worked across the stack. I've also interned at Symsoft Solutions as well as Signata Corp.

After graduating I want to work as a data scientist to solve problems with data pertaining to health. In my long term future, I want to work in venture capital, helping founders realize their vision and help make great companies.

My strongest programming language is Python, followed by Java and C. I can also be productive in C++ and JavaScript.

My biggest interests in computer science are based around machine learning and distributed systems. Most of my programming work within machine learning has been in python. I love the simplicity of the language and the plethora of libraries available. Within distributed systems, I've experimented writing MapReduce jobs on Hadoop, and setting up clusters with the HDFS.

I'm also picking up Golang so I can develop distributed applications. Go is great for this because of its strong concurrency model. One of my projects to pick up Go is writing a NoSQL database.
